<div class="mdx-editorial-lines">

<p>I am a shifting combination of identities,<br />
interfaces through which I move through the world.<br />
Each one as much a performance as it is a truth.<br />
As much a choice as it is an inheritance.</p>

<p>My personal identity comes first<br />
who I am in the kitchen, at the dinner table,<br />
in the voices that call me by my childhood name.<br />
Family holds the longest memory of me.<br />
They remember versions of me I have forgotten.</p>

<p>And then there are friends,<br />
the family I found by accident and kept by choice.<br />
They know me not from blood but from presence.<br />
From late nights and shared silences,<br />
from the version of me that showed up when no one was watching.<br />
Friends hold a different kind of memory<br />
not who I was born as, but who I grew into.<br />
They are mirrors I chose to stand in front of.</p>

<p>Together, family and friends form the foundation.
Not a single state, but a layered one.
A root system I keep returning to.</p>

<p>On top of that, a professional self,<br />
built from skill and discipline,<br />
shaped by every team I've worked within,<br />
every tool I've learned to use.<br />
This identity I cultivate carefully.<br />
It earns me my place in the room.</p>

<p>Then there is my Maratha identity.<br />
A warrior lineage carried now in quieter ways<br />
in stubbornness, in pride, in a refusal to fold.<br />
Maharashtra lives in the older generation's Marathi,<br />
in the prayers said a certain way,<br />
in the food prepared without a recipe,<br />
passed hand to hand for generations.<br />
The roots are there, even when the soil has shifted.</p>

<p>I grew up in Vadodara.<br />
Gujarat shaped my streets, my rhythm, my virtues.<br />
I learned to bargain in Gujarati,<br />
to read the city's pace before I could name it.<br />
Baroda is colonial, Vadodara is mine.<br />
A city of art schools and old palaces,<br />
of Navratri and garba and late monsoon evenings.<br />
Never fully one thing. Always something between.</p>

<p>My national identity is how the world files me.<br />
Indian. It is a passport. A checkbox.<br />
It is 1.4 billion people collapsed into a single word.<br />
To be Indian is to live inside contradiction<br />
ancient and urgent.<br />
devotion and ambition.<br />
caste and aspiration.<br />
tradition and disruption.<br />
It is to know what jugaad means in your bones.<br />
To carry both pride and grief for the same place.<br />
To love a country that is still becoming itself.</p>

<p>The self is not a fixed thing.<br />
The body presents a consistent image,<br />
but the body itself is always in flux.<br />
Outside I am simply Indian.<br />
The label shrinks and expands depending on who is watching.</p>

<p>I am not a combination of these identities.<br />
I am the one who moves between them.<br />
The operating system beneath the interfaces.<br />
A set of values navigating all these selves.<br />
I am what I hold onto when the labels fall away.<br />
I am the stubbornness of Maratha blood<br />
softened by Gujarat's warmth.<br />
I am as much as I allow myself to be.<br />
I am who I try to be.<br />
I am who I end up being.</p>
